Pedestrian Accidents in Rome

An exploration of accident hotspots and patterns across the city, combining spatial clustering with contextual features.

Study Summary

Analysis of pedestrian accidents in Rome.

Data from Opendata Comune di Roma, filtered to accidents involving one vehicle. Accidents took place during the period 01/01/2013 – 31/08/2022. Accidents with missing information have been removed.

Injury Levels

  • 0 — uninjured (but involved in the accident)
  • 1 — slight injury
  • 2 — moderate injury (hospitalised)
  • 3 — moderate/severe injury, died later
  • 4 — died instantly
2013–2022 Comune di Roma Open Data One-vehicle accidents

Context

Use the interactive maps below to explore the pedestrian accident hotspots in Rome or browse every recorded one vehicle – pedestrian accident in the study period.

Spatial clustering (DBSCAN) was used to combine accident frequencies and pedestrian injury severities to pinpoint pedestrian “blackspots”.

Accident data taken from Open Data Comune di Roma: Incidenti stradali nel territorio di Roma Capitale.
